Partnership strategy
The best partners sit close to the moment a pet owner needs help.

Use month one to prove which local handoffs turn into booked visits. Bigger partners are easier after that proof exists.

The filter
If the idea starts with "they also serve pet owners," it has not earned a place on the page yet.

A partner needs a care moment, a decision point, or a measurable handoff. Same customer is thin. Same category is thin. A famous logo is thin. The useful question is simpler: what would the pet owner do next because this partner mentioned Zoomies?

Partnership sorting map
Sort partners by the handoff they create.

A local groomer, FirstVet, Petplan, and Rover should never sit in the same priority list. They move at different speeds, need different proof, and solve different problems for Zoomies.

01

Move this month

Owner-led or product-led partners with a clear care moment and little procurement.

  • Local groomers
  • Puppy trainers
  • Dog daycares
  • Independent pet shops
  • BorrowMyDoggy
  • FirstVet referral pilot

Relationships that make Zoomies feel like a serious care provider with reliable clinic access.

  • Petplan partner route
  • Hello Vet market conversation
  • Selected rescue centres
  • Out-of-hours referral partners
03

Earn later

Large partners worth approaching after Zoomies has data from smaller tests.

  • Butternut Box
  • Rover
  • Dogs Trust
  • Battersea
  • ManyPets
  • Build-to-rent operators
04

Leave out for now

Tempting on paper, muddy in the actual customer journey.

  • Waggel as a first-90-days play
  • Generic pet brand email swaps
  • National platforms with no UK contact
  • Anything that only offers awareness
First 30 days
Start where a clinic can actually take the demand.

The first month should create proof near existing clinics. No long decks. No broad brand pitches. Find the local businesses that already get asked for vet recommendations, then track whether their clients book and visit.

Week 1

Map the catchments

For each clinic, list every groomer, trainer, daycare, pet shop, dog walker group, rescue contact, and dog-friendly workplace within one mile. Add owner name, footfall clue, and distance from the clinic.

Week 2

Visit 15 local partners

Bring a small counter card, a QR code, and one line they can say to clients: "Zoomies is the vet membership down the road, first month free with our code." No pitch deck.

Week 3

Open two bigger doors

Send a useful referral note to FirstVet and a safety guide idea to BorrowMyDoggy. Give them something that helps their users before asking for placement.

Week 4

Read the behaviour

Review sign-ups, booked visits, first consults, postcode, clinic capacity, cancellation reasons, and source. Keep partners sending members who book and visit. Drop the rest quickly.

FirstVet

Digital vet consults often end with an in-person care need. Zoomies can be the London clinic handoff for non-emergency primary-care cases.
Move this month
Why this partner

FirstVet already owns the moment where an owner has described the symptoms, uploaded photos, spoken to a vet, and been told a clinic visit is needed. That owner should not have to start again on Google.

Zoomies works if the member understands access. A referral after a video consult is a clean way to show that access in a real care moment.

First ask
  • London-only pilot for non-emergency cases that need physical follow-up
  • Referral page with clinic locations, monthly price, and booking route
  • Clinical notes passed with the owner where appropriate
  • Monthly review of referred cases and booked visits
Contact route

Rob Mitchell or the UK commercial lead is the right starting point. If that is too senior, ask who owns clinic referral pathways in the UK.

Likely blocker: confidence that Zoomies can take the case quickly and give owners a clear next step.

BorrowMyDoggy

A trust-led dog owner network with a very human care problem: people often look after dogs they do not own.
Founder-led
Why this partner

BorrowMyDoggy is useful because it sits inside real dog care. A borrower notices a limp, vomiting, a rash, or a dog that suddenly seems flat. They need a calm answer and the owner needs to know there is a vet option nearby.

This should feel like a safety resource first. The commercial side follows if members use it.

First ask

  • Add Zoomies as the primary-care option for London members
  • Use one tracked code and one landing page
  • Offer a short vet Q&A for members if the guide gets used

Likely blocker: anything that feels too sales-led for the tone of the member base.

Local groomers, trainers, and daycares

The least glamorous section, and probably the fastest way to learn which local sources create members who visit.
Field sprint
Why this partner

Groomers see skin, ears, coats, lumps, weight changes, and anxious dogs every week. Trainers see first-time owners trying to do the right thing. Daycares see repeat behaviour. These are people owners already ask for advice.

The test is small enough to start on Monday and concrete enough to measure by postcode.

First ask
  • Counter card with unique QR code
  • Referral fee after activated membership, rather than sign-up
  • Clinic open evening for local pet businesses
  • Named recommendations back to the partner where clinically appropriate
Contact route

Owner or manager, in person. The pitch should take two minutes. If they ask for details, send the one-page follow-up after the visit.

Likely blocker: admin burden. Keep it to a card, a code, and a simple payment rule.

Zigzag

A puppy-training app belongs on the list because it sits near the first-care window, when owners are forming habits and asking basic health questions.
Small test only
Why this partner

Puppy owners are already spending time and money to avoid mistakes. The vet decision is part of the same period. The right version is small: a London puppy health checklist before any major product integration.

This also teaches Zoomies which puppy-owner messages turn into consults, which matters for paid social and search too.

First ask

  • One offer code for first month free
  • Vet Q&A if the checklist gets downloads
  • Review clicks and puppy consults after 30 days

Lorna Winter is the right first contact if the asset touches training content. Patrick Luke matters if it becomes a bigger commercial test.

Petplan

Useful for credibility and partner infrastructure. Too slow for a week-one acquisition bet.
Credibility route
Why this partner

Petplan has deep relationships with vets and animal charities. A credible route with them would help Zoomies look more established to pet owners and investors.

The first step should be learning the partner criteria before proposing any campaign. A serious insurer will care about clinical standards, complaints, claims confusion, and whether the clinic can support the demand.

First ask
  • Understand the clinic partner criteria
  • Ask whether Zoomies can qualify for a London partner route
  • Prepare clinic coverage, NPS, retention, and care model notes
  • Keep marketing out of the first message
Contact route

Colin Young is the strongest route if partner conversion is the question. Milly Goddard or Victoria White become relevant if the route turns into partner marketing.

Likely blocker: formal process and any confusion between insurance and membership.

Hello Vet

Competitor watch, market learning, and possible referral logic if the clinical boundary is clean.
Handle carefully
Why this partner

Hello Vet is worth studying because it shows where modern vet expectations are going: transparent pricing, polished clinics, WhatsApp-style access, and a clear owner experience.

A referral route only makes sense if Zoomies and Hello Vet agree which care each side should own. Primary membership and complex case handling may separate cleanly. They may also collide. The page should admit that.

First ask
  • No cold partnership pitch
  • Use a warm route if one exists
  • Start with market conversation and case boundaries
  • Involve clinical leadership before anything reaches members
Contact route

James Lighton, Dr Oli Viner, or Alessandro Guazzi only through a warm route. This is a founder or operator conversation. A cold BD email would be the wrong route.

Likely blocker: customer confusion and competitive sensitivity.

Bigger partners to earn later
Some names belong on the bench until Zoomies has proof.

These names need proof before they get a cold email from a six-clinic company.

Butternut Box

There is already a Full Fluff Exam relationship. I would measure that before proposing anything larger.

  • Track impressions, clicks, sign-ups, activation, first visit, and retention
  • Compare CAC against paid social and search
  • Return with data before asking for a portal placement or bigger campaign

Rover

Good category fit, slow path. Blackstone ownership and European expansion make it less likely to move for a small UK test without proof.

  • Watch for UK or EU product hires
  • Use smaller sitter and daycare tests first
  • Pitch only when there is evidence that dog-care partners create booked visits

Battersea and Dogs Trust

The adoption handover is a strong care moment, but charity partnerships move through a process.

  • Approach through adopter welfare and fundraising
  • Use a donation per activated member
  • Prepare a one-page proposal ready for internal review

ManyPets

Better as a category learning and intro route than a cold partnership target.

  • Use Steven Mendel as an advisor-style intro if there is a warm path
  • Avoid broad insurer member-benefit pitches
  • Keep insurance and primary-care membership clearly separated

Build-to-rent and employers

Useful once local proof exists. Pet-friendly flats and offices can create dense pockets of members near clinics.

  • Start with buildings within clinic catchments
  • Offer a simple pet-owner benefit
  • Measure sign-ups by building and clinic capacity

Waggel

I would leave Waggel out of the first 90 days. It sounds tempting because insured pet owners already pay monthly, but the customer message gets muddy.

  • Insurance, routine care, membership, claims, and exclusions are too much to explain in one partner placement
  • The next action for the owner is unclear
  • FirstVet is a cleaner handoff. Petplan is a cleaner credibility route.

Measurement
A partner has not worked until a member books, visits, and stays.

Every partner gets the same tracking spine

  • Source code
  • Partner landing page
  • QR code if offline
  • Postcode and nearest clinic
  • Sign-up
  • First booking
  • First visit
  • 30-day retention
  • Cancellation reason
  • Partner follow-up date
  • Cost per activated member

The useful question is: did this source send people who used the clinic?

If a partner sends sign-ups that do not book, it is a messaging problem. If they book and do not show, it is a handoff problem. If they visit and cancel, it is a product or expectation problem. The numbers should tell us which one.

Founder note
I would use the first month to make the bigger conversations easier.

Start near existing clinics: groomers, trainers, daycares, FirstVet, and BorrowMyDoggy. Keep the partners that create booked visits.

The order matters. Local proof first. Clinical handoffs next. Larger partners after there is something worth showing them.
