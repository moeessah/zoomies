Social Strategy
Content that earns
its place in the feed.

84% of London pet owners delayed a vet visit because of money. The content has one job: make £33/month feel obvious before someone even visits the site. No one follows a pet brand for product updates. They follow it because it makes them feel smart, seen, or like they need to send it to someone right now.

Instagram + TikTok
"What actually happened at the vet today"
One story, one vet, phone camera, no script. The vet looks like someone you’d trust, not someone performing trust. Posts like people tell stories to friends. One per week, without exception. This gives the account a repeat face and a repeat reason to come back.
  • "A golden retriever came in absolutely convinced he was dying. He was constipated. His owner nearly cried."
  • "She stared at her dog limping for nine weeks because she didn’t want to Google the bill. We fixed it in one visit."
  • "47 consultations today. One of them made the whole team go quiet. Here’s what happened."
  • "A cat who’s been here 14 times this year. Her owner calls her ‘my most expensive cheap decision.’"
Instagram Carousels
"The Vet Bill Breakdown"
Real anonymised bills, line by line. What each item actually costs to provide. Slide 7 is always what a Zoomies member paid. The anger builds, then lands somewhere useful. Earns saves and reaches far beyond existing followers. The series that makes strangers care.
  • "This dog owner paid £340 for an ear infection. Swipe to see where the money went."
  • "The ‘prescription fee’ line. What it is. Why every vet charges it. Why we don’t."
  • "12 London vet bills, one procedure, six different prices. The range will annoy you."
  • "One member’s before/after. Year one data. The number surprised even us."
TikTok + Reels
"Vet reads your Google searches"
Named Zoomies vet, on camera, reading real pet health questions submitted by followers. The answers are real. The questions are often unhinged. Format already proven in US vet accounts. Nobody in UK vet space is doing it. One per week, 30 seconds, named vet every time.
  • "My dog ate an entire wheel of brie. Is this fine." (Real question. Real answer.)
  • "My cat has been staring at the same wall for 45 minutes. Is he meditating or dying."
  • "What a dog’s gum colour tells a vet in 3 seconds" - the one that gets saved 800 times
  • "When to actually panic about your cat vomiting. When not to." - every cat owner sends this
Instagram Stories + Reels
"Zoomies at 8pm on a Sunday"
Every Monday: one anonymised weekend WhatsApp exchange. Time, question, answer, and what the owner avoided. It shows someone got help at 8pm on a Sunday and did not end up in an emergency vet at £400. No other vet can run this format.
  • "Sunday 9:14pm. ‘She’s been licking her paw for two hours, should I be worried?’ Here’s what Dr Rachel said."
  • "Last Sunday after 6pm: 47 messages answered. 12 appointments booked. 3 people who didn’t go to emergency."
  • "The message that came in at 11:47pm. The answer that took 4 minutes. The owner who slept."
LinkedIn
"Building Zoomies in public"
Marcus’s voice. Honest about what’s hard. Specific about what the numbers actually say. The one post format that earns press, investor attention, and the kind of hire who self-selects for a company that tells the truth. Not corporate communications. A founder talking like a person.
  • "We assumed people joined to save money. The number one thing they tell us is they joined to stop dreading the vet."
  • "We opened in the US. Week one was humbling. Here’s every number and what we’re changing."
  • "I stopped explaining the model. I just show people the bill now. Here’s what changed."
  • "Our NPS is over 70. I was not ready for what that would mean operationally."
Instagram · Evergreen
"Your dog is telling the vet something you can’t hear"
Vets read signals owners walk past every day. This series gives one away per post. High saves, broad reach, works as well in month 12 as month 1. Breed-specific versions get extra reach from hashtags. The format that builds the channel between launch moments.
  • "The first thing a vet checks when your dog walks in. It’s not what you think."
  • "If you have a Labrador, your vet already knows what they’re going to find. Here’s what we look for."

Getting Members to Post for Us
Six mechanics. All opt-in. None of them feel like a campaign.

Members with a 70+ NPS already post unprompted. The job is to make it easy and give them a reason at exactly the right moment. The prompts below have the actual words, not the category description.

01
The zero-effort ask
Stories sticker after every first appointment. The prompt: "Somewhere in London, a dog just had their first free check-up and is about to get extra sofa privileges. Show us. 🐾" No prize. Just a reason to post at the peak moment of goodwill.
02
The before/after bill
Ask members to DM their old vet bill. Anonymise it, post it as a Breakdown slide. Most people have one saved in their email. The prompt: "Got a vet bill from before you joined? We’ll anonymise it and post it. Let the number do the talking."
03
The 12-month card
At every member anniversary: an auto-generated card. Pet’s name, total consultations, estimated saving vs London average. Example: "Clementine: 8 visits. £0 in extras. Estimated saving: £620. Happy anniversary. 🐾" The saving number is what gets screenshotted and posted.
04
The referral that doesn’t feel like a referral
Every member gets a permanent referral link. One free month per join, no expiry, no minimum. The prompt to share it: nothing. People who’ve had a good experience want to tell someone. The financial incentive makes the ask self-explanatory without us having to make it.

Twitter/XTikTok Duet
Long-game series
One pet. One year. Twelve posts.
Find one willing member. Follow their pet for 12 months. One post per month. What happened at the vet, what it cost (£33), what they were worried about, what the vet said. By month 12 there’s a full narrative arc that’s been compounding for a year. This is what Humans of New York understood: the serialised single-subject story earns loyalty nothing else does. Followers who joined in month 4 go back and read months 1-3.
  • Month 1: Ruby joins. Her cat Clementine has a limp. "We were terrified of what it would cost."
  • Month 4: Clementine needs dental work. Included in membership. Ruby’s reaction: one screenshot.
  • Month 12: "A year with Clementine, a year with Zoomies." Engagement peaks because followers are attached to the outcome.

What Gets Cut
Every series has a number. If it misses, it goes.

Each series gets a number and a stop date. These are the 60-day calls.

Series 01
"What happened at the vet today"
Win: Avg 8%+ profile-visit rate from Reels at week 8
Iterate: Below 8% but saves strong - clip the format, try shorter
Kill if: Profile visits flat and no engagement lift after 8 posts. The vet isn’t connecting. Try a different vet or format before cutting entirely.
Series 02
Vet Bill Breakdown
Win: 3%+ save rate per carousel, new follower spike on post days
Iterate: Good saves, low shares - the story arc isn’t landing, rework the slide order
Kill if: Save rate under 1% at week 6. The anger isn’t converting to action. Test a softer bill (dental vs ear) before cutting.
Series 03
Vet Reads Your Google Searches
Win: 500+ new followers per video within 72hrs by week 6
Iterate: Good watch time, flat follows - the hook isn’t specific enough, lean into weirder questions
Kill if: Watch time under 40% at week 4. The vet isn’t watchable. Change vets or change format to text-over rather than face-to-camera.
Series 04
Zoomies at 8pm Sunday
Win: Story reply rate 2%+ and DMs referencing the format
Iterate: Low replies but people are screenshot-sharing - post as a static feed post instead
Kill if: No engagement lift from Stories at week 6. Members aren’t seeing it. Try a feed version.
Series 05
Breeds as Coworkers
Win: 20%+ of engagement is tags (people tagging specific friends)
Iterate: High likes, low tags - the gag isn’t specific enough. More workplace detail.
Kill if: Less than 10% tag rate at week 8. The mechanic isn’t firing. The whole premise relies on people seeing a specific person in it.
Series 06
Building Zoomies in Public (LinkedIn)
Win: 2+ press mentions or inbound requests from posts by month 3
Iterate: High engagement, no press - the posts are too internal, add a bigger industry observation
Kill if: No inbound at month 3 and engagement flat. Marcus’s voice isn’t landing. Ghost-write one post with a professional and compare.
